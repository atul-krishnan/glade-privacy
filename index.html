Blade — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 17 July 2026

Blade (“the app”) is a digital-wellbeing app that helps you pause before opening distracting apps. This policy explains what the app does and does not do with your information.

Blade does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. There are no accounts, no analytics, no advertising, and no servers. Everything the app does happens entirely on your device.

Information Blade accesses

To do its job, Blade uses these Android permissions:

  • Accessibility service — Blade is notified when an app moves to the foreground so it can show a pause screen for the apps you chose to limit. Blade only reads the package name of the app in the foreground. It does not read the contents of your screen, your messages, what you type, your passwords, or any data inside other apps.
  • Display over other apps — lets Blade show its pause screen on top of the app you opened.
  • Notifications — Blade posts on-device notifications only: an ongoing screen-time/session line, and optional daily check-in and wind-down reminders you can turn off in Settings. These are generated on your device from your own usage; nothing is sent anywhere.

The list of apps you choose to limit, your pause and reminder settings, your appearance (light/dark) choice, and your on-device usage counts are stored only on your device (in the app’s local storage). This information is never sent anywhere and is removed when you uninstall the app.

Information Blade collects and shares

  • Collected: none.
  • Shared with third parties: none.
  • Sold: never.

Because no data leaves your device, there is nothing for us to access, retain, or disclose.

Children

Blade does not knowingly collect any data from anyone, including children.
